What is Cowork? Frontier

Microsoft 365 Copilot Cowork carries out tasks on your behalf — across Microsoft 365.

You describe what you need in natural language, and Cowork does the work — sending emails, scheduling meetings, creating documents, posting in Teams, and managing files. It can plan a multi-step task, ask you for clarification, run skills in sequence or parallel, and pause for your approval before any action that affects the outside world.

Source: Get started with Cowork (Frontier) — Microsoft Support

What Cowork can do

📧 Outlook

Send emails, reply to messages, forward entire threads with attachments. Save drafts, manage attachments before sending.

📅 Calendar

Schedule meetings using natural language. Add Teams meeting links. Manage your calendar.

📄 Documents

Create and edit Word documents, Excel spreadsheets, PowerPoint presentations and PDFs.

💬 Teams

🔍 Search

Search across your organization for files, people and information.

📁 Files

Browse and manage files in OneDrive and SharePoint.

🔬 Deep Research

Conduct deep research across multiple sources and compile comprehensive analysis on complex topics.

🌅 Daily Briefing & meeting prep

Prepare daily briefings and meeting intelligence summaries.

📣 Communications

Draft stakeholder communications such as status updates and announcements.

⏰ Scheduled prompts

Run prompts on a schedule so recurring tasks happen automatically — daily, weekly or monthly.

How it's different from regular Copilot

Regular Copilot

You're in an app — Word, Excel, Outlook — and Copilot helps you with that one thing. Single surface, single artefact.

Cowork

You delegate a task with multiple steps and outputs across surfaces. Cowork plans the work, runs the skills, asks for approval before sending or posting, and shows you progress as it goes.

The shape of a Cowork session

  1. You describe the task in natural language — up to 16,000 characters. Attach files, use voice input, or pick a starter prompt.
  2. Cowork plans and shows a thinking indicator + skill messages ("Preparing to compose emails", "Creating your presentation").
  3. It may ask for clarification — pick from suggested choices or skip.
  4. It approves before acting — sending email, posting Teams message, scheduling meeting. Each approval shows a preview and a risk indicator.
  5. Files appear in the side panel — Input, Output, Skills used, Permissions granted, Schedule.
  6. You can pause, resume or cancel the run at any time.
  7. You can schedule the prompt to run again — daily, weekly, on a cadence.

📌 Always review before approving

AI-generated documents, emails and messages should be treated as drafts. Always verify recipients, content and parameters in the approval dialog before letting Cowork send or post anything on your behalf.

Tips before you start

  • Be specific. Cowork can misinterpret broad instructions. Name the deliverables, the audiences, and the tone.
  • Attach the source. Drag a file in, or pick from OneDrive / SharePoint / Teams.
  • Approve carefully. Read each preview before approving an email, post or invite.
  • Use voice. The microphone button is faster than typing for long prompts.
  • Pause whenever. You can pause mid-step, redirect, and resume.

The Cowork Demo ★

One prompt. Six deliverables. Across four surfaces. In parallel.

🎯 The scenario

It's Monday. Siew Ling has the Quarterly Board Operations Review on Friday. By close of business Wednesday she needs an executive summary, a sustainability briefing note, a Board deck, an email to her division heads, a calendar invite for a follow-up review, and a heads-up Teams message to the Plantation Operations leadership channel.

Last quarter, this list took her team three full days. Today, we'll watch Cowork knock most of it out in a single conversation.

Steps

  1. Open m365.cloud.microsoft and select Cowork from the Agents pane.
  2. Attach 02_Contoso_OpsReport.docx (drag-and-drop, or "Attach cloud files" from OneDrive).
  3. Paste the master prompt below.
  4. Watch the side panel — Cowork loads skills (Word, Communications, Email, Calendar, PowerPoint), and files start arriving in the Output folder.
  5. Approve each external action — email send, calendar invite, Teams post — only after reviewing the preview.
  6. Refine any draft inline, then ship.

The master Cowork prompt

I am Siew Ling, Group COO at Contoso Plantation Berhad. The Quarterly Board Operations Review is on Friday. Using the attached Q1 Group Operations Report as the source of truth, prepare these SIX deliverables in parallel: 1. WORD — "Q1 2025 Operations Executive Summary" • One page, board-grade. Structure: Headline / Plantation / Manufacturing / Property / Sustainability / Top 3 Risks. • Calm, factual tone. ~350 words. 2. WORD — "Sustainability Briefing Note · Q1 2025" • Two pages. • Cover RSPO recertification, MSPO compliance, EUDR readiness for the EU Deforestation Regulation. • Highlight the Liberia operations gap and propose a remediation timeline. • End with three questions the Board should expect from the Sustainability Sub-committee. 3. POWERPOINT — "Q1 2025 Operations Review · Board Pack" • 10-12 slides. Conservative green/cream palette. No emojis. • Title / Executive summary / Plantation OER vs target / CRITICAL estates (Temerloh, Simalungun) / Manufacturing utilisation & margin / Contoso Land launches & take-up / Sustainability scorecard / Top 3 risks / Action items / Asks of the Board. 4. EMAIL — to my four division heads (Plantation, Manufacturing, Property, Sustainability) • Subject line punchy and specific. • Reminds them Board pack pre-reads close end of Wednesday. • Asks each to confirm two slides and one risk callout. • Warm but firm tone, signed "Siew Ling". DRAFT only — do not send until I approve. 5. CALENDAR INVITE — "Liberia Operations Follow-up · Tuesday next week" • 60 minutes, 9:00 AM Kuala Lumpur time. Add a Teams meeting link. • Invite the Plantation Director, Liberia Country Manager, Group Sustainability Lead. • Agenda in the body: yield gap, EUDR readiness, capex pipeline, ask of the Board. 6. TEAMS MESSAGE — to the "Plantation Operations Leadership" channel • Three short paragraphs, conversational but professional. • Heads-up that Friday's Board review will spotlight Temerloh and Simalungun. • Asks Regional Heads to come prepared with a one-line operational hypothesis. • Ends on a positive note about Lahad Datu's strong quarter. DRAFT only — do not post until I approve. Keep terminology consistent across all six artefacts: OER, FFB/ha, RSPO, MSPO, EUDR, Contoso Land. Where you cite numbers, use the figures from the attached report.

What to watch in the side panel

📊 Progress

The progress bar climbs as Cowork completes each sub-task. You can read the step-by-step log: "Composing your email", "Creating your presentation".

📁 Output folder

Files appear as they're created — Word, Word, PowerPoint. Each has Download and Preview buttons. Use Download All at the top to grab them as a zip.

🔧 Skills

Watch the skills list grow: Word, PowerPoint, Email, Calendar Management, Communications. Cowork loads them as needed.

🛡️ Permissions

Each external action — send, post, schedule — requires explicit approval with a preview and a risk indicator. You can Approve & Remember to skip prompts for similar actions later in the conversation.

The "before / after"

Before Cowork

Six separate drafting sessions across two days — open Word, draft, save, attach, switch to Outlook, write the email, switch to Teams, write the message, schedule the meeting, brief the EA, repeat.

With Cowork

One prompt. Six drafts in parallel in a few minutes. The next half-hour is review and refinement — not first-draft drudgery. The team gets time back for what humans actually do best: judgement and conversation.

📅 Want this every quarter? Schedule it.

You can ask Cowork to run a similar prompt automatically on a cadence. For example:

Every quarter, two weeks before the scheduled Board Operations Review date in my calendar, draft the Q-end executive summary, sustainability briefing and Board deck from the latest Group Operations Report in /Documents/Board/.

Limitations · what to expect

  • Cowork can only access data your Microsoft 365 account is authorised to use.
  • Cowork cannot access local files — work from OneDrive, SharePoint or Teams.
  • Cowork cannot delete files or folders in OneDrive or SharePoint.
  • Search results may be incomplete if source data is outdated or not indexed.
  • Complex multi-step tasks with many dependencies may not always complete as expected — pause, redirect, resume.
  • AI-generated artefacts are drafts. Always review before approving send or share.
  • Custom skills are not validated by Microsoft — review their outputs carefully.

Prompting Tips for Cowork

A simple framework — and a Group-flavoured prompt library you can take home.

The GCSE framework

Every good Cowork prompt has four parts. Get these right and Cowork stops guessing.

Goal What do you want? Be specific about every output. "Draft these six deliverables in parallel…" — not "Help with my Board prep".
Context Who are you, who's the audience, what's the stakes? "I'm Group COO preparing the Quarterly Board pack."
Source What should Cowork ground the answer in? Attach a file, link a SharePoint doc, or paste the data in.
Expectation Format, tone, length, structure — and say "draft only — do not send until I approve" for any external action.

Worked example

I am Siew Ling, Group COO at Contoso Plantation Berhad, preparing for the Quarterly Board Operations Review. From the attached Q1 Operations Report, draft three artefacts in parallel: a one-page executive summary, a sustainability briefing note, and an email to my four division heads. Use only the figures and risks in the document — no external sources. Tone: calm, factual, board-grade for the docs; warm but firm for the email. Email is draft only — do not send until I approve.

Cowork prompt patterns

Pattern 1 · Parallel deliverables

Using [ATTACHED_SOURCE], prepare in parallel: 1. WORD — [NAME & one-line purpose] 2. POWERPOINT — [NAME & one-line purpose] 3. EMAIL — [RECIPIENTS], subject "[SUBJECT_LINE]", tone [TONE] 4. CALENDAR — [TITLE], [LENGTH], attendees [ATTENDEES], [TIMEZONE] 5. TEAMS — channel "[CHANNEL_NAME]", [LENGTH], tone [TONE] Save outputs to /[FOLDER_PATH]/. Keep terminology consistent across all artefacts. Drafts only for external actions.

Pattern 2 · Scheduled recurring task

Every [CADENCE] at [TIME] [TIMEZONE], do [TASK] using files from /[SOURCE_FOLDER]/[PERIOD]/. Output: 1. [ARTEFACT_1] — [WHERE_TO_SAVE] 2. [ARTEFACT_2] — [WHERE_TO_SAVE] 3. [ARTEFACT_3] — [WHERE_TO_SAVE] Notify me in the "[CHANNEL_NAME]" Teams channel when ready.

Pattern 3 · Deep research → brief

Conduct deep research on [TOPIC] with focus on [GROUP_LENS] over the last [TIME_WINDOW]. Output as a Word brief titled "[TOPIC] — Group Brief for [DATE]" with sections: Headline, What we know, What we don't, Implications for the Group, Recommended next step. Cite source URLs as footnotes throughout. Save to /Group/Research/[TOPIC]/.

Pattern 4 · Triage and respond

From unread emails in my inbox tagged [CATEGORY] over the last 48 hours: 1. Group them into three buckets: Needs my decision, Can be delegated, FYI only. 2. For each "Needs my decision", draft a one-line summary plus a suggested Outlook response in my voice. 3. For each "Can be delegated", draft an Outlook forward email to [DELEGATE] with a one-line ask. Drafts only — wait for my approval before sending anything.

Pattern 5 · Cross-divisional rollup

Build the Group rollup for [QUARTER] [YEAR]. From the latest segment reports in /Group/Q-end/[QUARTER]/ for Plantation, Manufacturing and Contoso Land: 1. Draft a Word 1-page narrative in the Group MD's house style. 2. Build a 4-slide PowerPoint summary deck. 3. Post a Teams update in the "Group Operations" channel with the three biggest movers across segments. 4. Save all artefacts to /Group/Q-end/[QUARTER]/Rollup/.

🌱 One last tip

Treat Cowork like a smart, fast graduate analyst who's just joined the team. Hand them a clear brief, name the deliverables, point to the source — and tell them not to send anything until you've reviewed it. Spend 60 seconds on a great prompt and you'll save 60 minutes on a great output.

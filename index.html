What is Cowork? Frontier

Microsoft 365 Copilot Cowork carries out tasks on your behalf — across Microsoft 365.

You describe what you need in natural language, and Cowork does the work — sending emails, scheduling meetings, creating documents, posting in Teams, and managing files. It can plan a multi-step task, ask you for clarification, run skills in sequence or parallel, and pause for your approval before any action that affects the outside world.

How it's different from regular Copilot

Regular Copilot

You're in an app — Word, Excel, Outlook — and Copilot helps you with that one thing. Single surface, single artefact.

Cowork

You delegate a task with multiple steps and outputs across surfaces. Cowork plans the work, runs the skills, asks for approval before sending or posting, and shows you progress as it goes.

The shape of a Cowork session

  1. You describe the task in natural language — up to 16,000 characters. Attach files, use voice input, or pick a starter prompt.
  2. Cowork plans and shows a thinking indicator + skill messages ("Preparing to compose emails", "Creating your presentation").
  3. It may ask for clarification — pick from suggested choices or skip.
  4. It approves before acting — sending email, posting Teams message, scheduling meeting. Each approval shows a preview and a risk indicator.
  5. Files appear in the side panel — Input, Output, Skills used, Permissions granted, Schedule.
  6. You can pause, resume or cancel the run at any time.
  7. You can schedule the prompt to run again — daily, weekly, on a cadence.

📌 Always review before approving

AI-generated documents, emails and messages should be treated as drafts. Always verify recipients, content and parameters in the approval dialog before letting Cowork send or post anything on your behalf.

Tips before you start

  • Be specific. Cowork can misinterpret broad instructions. Name the deliverables, the audiences, and the tone.
  • Attach the source. Drag a file in, or pick from OneDrive / SharePoint / Teams.
  • Approve carefully. Read each preview before approving an email, post or invite.
  • Use voice. The microphone button is faster than typing for long prompts.
  • Pause whenever. You can pause mid-step, redirect, and resume.

POWERPOINT — "Q1 2025 Operations Review · Board Pack" • 10-12 slides. Conservative green/cream palette. EMAIL — to my four division heads (Industrial Operations, Manufacturing, Property, Sustainability) • Subject line punchy and specific. • Reminds them Board pack pre-reads close end of Wednesday. • Asks each to confirm two slides and one risk callout. • Warm but firm tone, signed "Siew Ling". The "before / after"

Before Cowork

Six separate drafting sessions across two days — open Word, draft, save, attach, switch to Outlook, write the email, switch to Teams, write the message, schedule the meeting, brief the EA, repeat.

With Cowork

One prompt. Six drafts in parallel in a few minutes. The next half-hour is review and refinement — not first-draft drudgery. The team gets time back for what humans actually do best: judgement and conversation.

The six core patterns

Pattern 1

Spreading + Parity Check

Pull raw source data into a company template, then re-check that the rebuilt totals tie back. Cowork verifies its own work before you do.

Pattern 2

Mixed-Format Extraction

Pull the same data out of PDFs, scanned images and Excel into one standardised schema. OCR low-confidence rows get flagged, not silently committed.

Pattern 3

Council of Agents

One source pack, five expert lenses in parallel. Each agent owns a viewpoint; a synthesis pass surfaces the dissent register.

Pattern 4

Recursive Entity Research

Drill down through corporate ownership until you reach natural persons or hit a depth limit. Every node carries a source URL and a verified-on date.

Pattern 5

Structured Pack with Citations

A fixed output format (sections A–I) plus a hard rule: cite note numbers, do not fabricate metrics. Missing data lands in the follow-up section.

Pattern 6

Model → Interactive App

Convert an Excel financial model into Python plus a self-contained HTML with sliders. The committee moves the assumptions in the room.

UC 1 Industrial Operations · Group Finance

Site Spreading + Parity Check

Three site monthly logs land in the Group Site Performance Template — then Cowork re-checks its own arithmetic and flags every variance.

Pattern · Spreading + Parity Check Apps · Excel · Word · SharePoint Complexity · Advanced

Sample source files

XLSXSite_Alpha_Mar2026.xlsxXLSXSite_Beta_Mar2026.xlsxXLSXSite_Gamma_Mar2026.xlsxXLSXGroup_Site_Performance_Template_v3.xlsx

Prompts

P1 Spread to template
I've attached three site monthly performance logs (Site Alpha, Site Beta, Site Gamma) and the Group Site Performance Template v3. Spread each site's data into the template's `Site Performance` sheet — one column per site. Map: throughput tonnage, yield %, active/developing acres, operator productivity (throughput tons/shift), consumables issued (kg/acre), diesel consumption (litres), water usage (m³), unit cost per throughput ton (USD). Capture multi-line manager commentary intact in the `Notes` column. Standardise dates to yyyy-mm-dd. Standardise units (tonnes, acres, mm, litres, USD). Don't perform any translation.
P2 Parity check
Now run a parity check on what you just spread: 1. For each site, sum throughput produced per week and confirm it ties to the monthly total in the source — flag any divergence > 2%. 2. Confirm yield calculated from (primary output produced / throughput processed) ties to the yield reported in the log — flag divergence > 0.3 percentage points. 3. Confirm consumables kg/acre × active acres ties to total consumables issued. 4. Cross-foot the template totals row. Produce `Parity_Check_Mar2026.xlsx` with one tab per site showing source value, template value, variance, and a Pass/Flag column.
P3 Brief the Operations MD
Write a one-page Word brief titled "Site Performance Spreading — March 2026 — Group Roll-up" for the Operations MD. Highlight: any flagged parity issues, the worst three KPI shifts vs February, and the three sites worth a site visit. Save the spreading workbook, parity check, and brief to /Operations/Monthly/Mar2026/.
What to watch
  • Three different site-log formats land in one canonical template.
  • Cowork re-checks its own arithmetic — finds its own errors before the MD does.
  • Multi-line manager commentary preserved, not flattened.
  • One single source of truth for the GM, with a parity log behind it.
Honest framing

Parity check assumes the source totals are themselves correct. If a manager's source total is wrong, Cowork agrees with it — humans still own the underlying field data. For new template versions, do a one-off sample run before production use.

UC 2 Manufacturing · Strategy

Capex Model → Interactive Scenario App

A 5-year Frankfurt specialty-chem capex model becomes a working app the IC can interact with — sliders, scenarios, parity report against Excel.

Pattern · Model → Interactive App Apps · Excel · Code Interpreter · PowerPoint Complexity · Advanced

Sample source files

XLSXDetroit_Capex_Model_FY27.xlsx

Prompts

P1 Understand + build the app
I've attached our 5-year Frankfurt specialty-chem capex model. First walk me through the structure — drivers, inputs, formulas, and any assumptions an investment committee will push back on. Then convert it into a Python program with an interactive HTML front-end: - Sliders for: WACC (8–14%), EUR/USD (1.05–1.15), raw input A feedstock USD/T (800–1,400), capex contingency (5–15%), opex escalation (0–6%). - Show: total 5-year capex, NPV, IRR, payback period, year-by-year cash-out chart, sensitivity heatmap (raw input A × WACC). Deliver runnable Python plus a self-contained HTML I can preview locally.
P2 Validate + run scenarios
Run the new app at the model's original assumptions and produce a parity report against the spreadsheet (capex by year, NPV, IRR, total outflow) — flag any divergence > USD 100k. Then run three scenarios: - Base (current): WACC 11%, USD 4.45, raw input A USD 1,050, contingency 8%. - Upside: WACC 9%, USD 4.30, raw input A USD 950, contingency 6%. - Downside: WACC 13%, USD 4.65, raw input A USD 1,250, contingency 12%. Summarise NPV swing and the top three line items driving variance.
P3 Pack for the IC
Build a 6-slide Investment Committee pack titled "Detroit Capacity Expansion FY27 — Sensitivity Pack": 1. Cover 2. Scenario table 3. NPV waterfall 4. Sensitivity heatmap 5. Key risks 6. Recommendation. Save to /Manufacturing/Detroit/FY27_Capex/. Apply the "Confidential — Board Pack" sensitivity label.
What to watch
  • Cowork reads every sheet, formula, and named range — not just the numbers visible on screen.
  • Generated code is version-controllable; the model becomes auditable.
  • The IC moves the sliders themselves in the room — no more "can you re-run it with WACC 12%?".
  • Parity report proves the rebuild is faithful before anyone trusts a new number.
Honest framing

Cowork delivers Python plus a self-contained HTML you run locally. Production deployment happens inside the customer's environment. The Excel model still owns the source of truth — the app proves the rebuild is faithful, not the hosting.

Prompts

P1 Convene the council
You are acting as a council of AI agents helping Contoso Land assess the investment merits of the Riverside Mixed-Use Phase 3 development. For each role below, assume the role completely and produce a long, audit-ready interim report. Roles: 1. Land Bank Analyst — deep analysis of land cost, plot ratio, GDV/GFA, demographic catchment, comparable transactions within 5 km, current absorption rates. Cite township masterplans where relevant. 2. Council quality is bounded by IM and DD pack quality — garbage in, structured garbage out. The council does not replace the deal team — it gives them five sharper drafts to argue with.

UC 4 Group Finance

Mixed-Format Bank Statement Extraction

Bank A text PDF, Bank B scanned PDF, HSBC mobile screenshot — all become one Excel split by month, sorted ascending, ready to reconcile to the GL.

Pattern · Mixed-Format Extraction Apps · Excel · PDF · Image Complexity · Intermediate

Sample source files

PDFBankA_E-Stmt_Mar2026.pdfPDFBankB_E-Stmt_Mar2026.pdfPNGHSBC_Stmt_Mar2026.png

Prompts

P1 Extract → standardise → split by month
Extract the contents of the three bank statements into one Excel file with the following columns: Page No, Bank, Transaction Date, Description, Debit, Credit, Running Balance, Currency, Source File. Standardise on yyyy-mmm-dd as the date format. Capture multi-line transaction details intact. Ignore all text that is not part of a transaction (logos, marketing banners, addresses, footers). Don't perform any translation. Split the transactions by month into different worksheets and within each worksheet, sort the transactions chronologically ascending.
P2 Reconcile to the GL
Now reconcile the workbook to the GL: - Pull the corresponding GL bank-account balances from /Finance/GL/Mar2026/. - Compare opening balance, closing balance, total debits, total credits per bank. - Output `Recon_Mar2026.xlsx` with one tab per bank: source vs GL, variance, status (Match / Investigate / Missing). Flag anything > USD 1,000.
What to watch
  • Three different formats — text PDF, scanned PDF, mobile screenshot — all land in the same standardised schema.
  • Multi-line descriptions stay intact; bank-side header noise is filtered out.
  • Date normalisation makes downstream reconciliation trivial.
  • One worksheet per month, sorted ascending — reconciliation-ready, not just an extract.
Honest framing

OCR quality on scanned or low-resolution images can produce edge-case errors on amounts and dates — Cowork flags low-confidence rows rather than silently committing them. Bank-specific layouts evolve; new statement templates may need a sample run before production use.

UC 5 Group Procurement · Compliance

Recursive UBO + Sanctions Pack

An Asia-Pacific tier-3 supplier consolidator's vendor application becomes a fully drilled UBO tree, sanctions sweep, and a single PDF for Procurement Committee.

Pattern · Recursive Entity Research Apps · Deep Research · Word · PDF · Excel Complexity · Advanced

Sample source files

PDFVendor_Onboarding_PT_Acme_Industrial.pdf

Prompts

P1 Build the UBO tree
Create an Ultimate Beneficial Ownership diagram for PT Acme Industrial Lestari (the vendor on the attached application). Recursively identify the UBO of each identified shareholder until you reach individual natural persons or until you reach a 5-level depth — whichever comes first. For each entity: - Pull entity data from the application package; for missing data, conduct deep research using publicly available sources (SEC EDGAR, Companies House, OpenCorporates, equivalent national registries, OFAC and EU consolidated lists, news archives). - For every node, capture: legal entity name, registration number, jurisdiction, % ownership, source URL, last-verified date. - Where ownership is unclear or sourced from only one publication, flag the node "Verification needed" — do not guess. Render as a hierarchical diagram (Mermaid syntax) and a tabular ownership ledger.
P2 Sanctions + adverse-media sweep
For every node in the UBO tree, run: - Sanctions screening against OFAC SDN, EU Consolidated List, UK HMT, MAS, FinCEN. - Adverse-media screening (last 36 months) for: environmental violations, land disputes, labour violations, corruption, regulatory fines. - Politically Exposed Person (PEP) check at the natural-person leaves. Output `Sanctions_Sweep.xlsx` with one row per check: entity, check type, source, hit/no-hit, snippet, link.
P3 Convert to KYC pack
Convert the UBO diagram, ownership ledger, and sanctions sweep into a single PDF titled "PT Acme Industrial Lestari — KYC Pack" for the Procurement Committee. Include: cover page, executive summary (recommendation + flag count), UBO diagram, ownership ledger, sanctions sweep, sources & methodology. Save to /Procurement/Vendors/PT_Acme_Industrial/KYC/. Apply the "Confidential — Procurement" sensitivity label.
What to watch
  • Recursive — Cowork keeps drilling until it reaches natural persons or hits the depth limit.
  • Every node carries a source URL and a last-verified date.
  • The committee gets one PDF, not 14 browser tabs.
  • "Verification needed" flags are explicit — Cowork refuses to invent ownership.
Honest framing

Public registries lag reality; some jurisdictions (e.g. nominee-friendly) will not surface natural persons. Cowork flags these rather than fabricates them. This is screening, not certification — it does not replace the formal AML/KYC vendor process.

UC 6 Group Risk · Treasury

Prompts

P1 Build the underwriting pack
You are acting as Contoso Group Credit Risk. Produce an underwriting memo for the counterparty using only the three attached audited financial statements (FY22, FY23, FY24). OUTPUT FORMAT (UNDERWRITING PACK): A. Executive Summary (Credit View — max 1 page) B. Financial Performance Analysis C. Balance Sheet & Liquidity Assessment D. Cash Flow Adequacy Review E.   • Output is structured — the same nine sections every time, every analyst.
  • Every claim is anchored to a note number, not floating in the air.
  • Cowork refuses to invent ratios — missing data lands in section I as a follow-up to management.
  • Section F surfaces accounting flags an analyst might miss on a 250-page filing.
Honest framing

Underwriting still requires a human credit officer; this is the analyst draft, not the final approval. Disclosure quality of the source PDFs caps how good section F can be.

Researcher · Critique Frontier

A multi-model deep-research architecture: one model generates, another reviews — like academic peer review at AI speed.

How it works

1 · Generator

One frontier model leads the heavy lifting — plans the task, iterates through retrieval across internal sources and the web, synthesises the evidence, and produces an initial draft report.

2 · Reviewer

A second frontier model takes the draft and acts as expert reviewer — running a structured, rubric-based evaluation. Not a second author. A reviewer.

3 · Enhanced report

The reviewer returns an enhanced final report — sharper claims, stronger evidence-grounding, fewer holes. You see one polished output; under the hood, two models agreed it was ready.

The rubric — three review dimensions

Source Reliability Reviewer prioritises reputable, authoritative, domain-appropriate sources. Throws out weak citations. Verifies that evidence is verifiable and contextually right.
Report Completeness Did the final report actually answer the question? Reviewer pushes for relevant, unique, missing-angle coverage. This is where most of Critique's quality lift comes from.
Strict Evidence Grounding Every key claim must be anchored to a reliable source with a precise citation. Conservative grounding — speculation gets cut. This is the headline trust uplift.

Researcher · Model Council Frontier

Two frontier models. Two complete reports. One judge. Surfaces where they agree, where they diverge, and what each saw that the other missed.

How it works

1 · Parallel reports

Council runs an Anthropic model and an OpenAI model simultaneously. Each one produces a full, standalone, end-to-end research report — not a fragment.

2 · Judge model

A dedicated judge model reads both reports and writes a distilled cover letter — what the two reports agree on, where they meaningfully diverge in magnitude / framing / interpretation, and the unique contributions of each.

3 · You decide

You see all three: Report A, Report B, and the cover letter. You're not consuming a black-box answer — you're seeing two perspectives and choosing which framing is most useful for your decision.

When to reach for Model Council (instead of Critique)

✅ Good fit

  • Strategic / directional questions where there's no single right answer.
  • High-stakes investment, divestment, or M&A reads.
  • Geopolitical or regulatory scenarios with multiple plausible interpretations.
  • Hostile-territory questions where you want to triangulate framing.
  • Anything where you'll defend the answer in front of a Board.

⚠️ Less of a fit

  • Questions with a single defensible answer (use Critique).
  • Tight, document-grounded synthesis where you need one polished output.
  • Operational / multi-app execution (use Cowork instead).
  • Time-sensitive briefs — Council takes longer because it runs two pipelines.

Prompting Tips for Cowork

A simple framework — and a Group-flavoured prompt library you can take home.

The GCSE framework

Every good Cowork prompt has four parts. Get these right and Cowork stops guessing.

Goal What do you want? Be specific about every output. "Draft these six deliverables in parallel…" — not "Help with my Board prep".
Context Who are you, who's the audience, what's the stakes? "I'm Group COO preparing the Quarterly Board pack."
Source What should Cowork ground the answer in? Attach a file, link a SharePoint doc, or paste the data in.
Expectation Format, tone, length, structure — and say "draft only — do not send until I approve" for any external action.

Worked example

I am Siew Ling, Group COO at Contoso Group, preparing for the Quarterly Board Operations Review. From the attached Q1 Operations Report, draft three artefacts in parallel: a one-page executive summary, a sustainability briefing note, and an email to my four division heads. Use only the figures and risks in the document — no external sources. Tone: calm, factual, board-grade for the docs; warm but firm for the email. Email is draft only — do not send until I approve.

Exercise 1 · Q1 Yield Analysis

Excel Analyst Agent · ~10 minutes

🎯 Objective

Use Copilot in Excel and the Analyst agent to investigate Q1 OER performance across Contoso's estates, identify the two estates pulling the average below target (20%), and produce a chart Siew Ling can drop into her Board pack.

Steps

  1. Open 01_Contoso_YieldData.xlsx and switch to the Yield Data sheet. Click the Copilot icon in the ribbon.
  2. Ask Copilot for a high-level read on Q1 performance — using the prompt below.
  3. Drill down on the worst performers using the follow-up prompt.
  4. Insert a chart for the Board deck.
  5. (Optional) Hand off to the Analyst agent in Microsoft 365 Chat for deeper statistical analysis.

Prompt 1 · High-level read

I am Lim Siew Ling, Group COO at Contoso Plantation Berhad, preparing for the quarterly Board Operations Review. Analyse this yield dataset and tell me: 1. Group-level Q1 OER versus our 20% target. 2. Top 3 and bottom 3 estates by OER. 3. Any country (Malaysia, Indonesia, Liberia) tracking materially below target. 4. Estates flagged as CRITICAL — why, and what's the OER gap to target? Present as a short executive summary, no more than 150 words, followed by a clean table.

Prompt 2 · Drill into the worst performers

For the two CRITICAL estates (Temerloh, Pahang and Simalungun, North Sumatra), compare: - Their OER versus the group average and our 20% target - Their FFB/ha versus the group average - Whether the two estates share a pattern (age profile, region, weather) Then suggest three operational hypotheses I should ask the Regional Heads about in the Board review.

Prompt 3 · Insert a chart

Create a column chart of Q1 OER % by estate, sorted descending, with a dashed reference line at the 20% target. Use a green palette.

What to notice

  • Copilot pulls a group-level OER without you writing AVERAGEIF or PIVOT formulas.
  • It correctly flags Temerloh (17.5%) and Simalungun (17.6%) as the two CRITICAL estates.
  • It identifies Lahad Datu, Sabah (21.3%) as the top performer — useful counter-example for the Board.
  • The Analyst agent can take this further: regression of OER vs. FFB/ha, age profile, rainfall — directly in chat.

Exercise 2 · Operations Reports

Word PowerPoint · ~12 minutes

🎯 Objective

Take the 7-section Q1 Group Operations Report and produce two Board-ready artefacts: a one-page executive summary in Word, and a draft Board update deck in PowerPoint — both anchored on the same source document.

Part A · Summarise the report (Word)

  1. Open 02_Contoso_OpsReport.docx and click the Copilot icon in Word.
  2. Ask Copilot to produce an executive summary using the prompt below.
  3. Then ask it to extract the action items separately so they can be tracked.

Prompt · Executive summary

Summarise this Q1 2025 Group Operations Report as a one-page executive summary for the Contoso Plantation Berhad Board. Use this structure: - Headline (2 sentences — what mattered this quarter) - Plantation: Q1 OER, top/bottom estates, headline risks - Manufacturing: oleochemicals utilisation, margin trend - Property (KLK Land): launches, take-up, receivables - Sustainability: RSPO, MSPO and EUDR status - Top 3 risks the Board needs to discuss Tone: factual, calm, board-grade. Maximum 350 words.

Prompt · Extract action items

From the meeting transcript section of this report, extract every action item as a table with columns: Owner, Action, Due date, Status. Sort by due date ascending.

Part B · Build the Board deck (PowerPoint)

  1. Open a new PowerPoint presentation and click Copilot → Create presentation from file.
  2. Reference 02_Contoso_OpsReport.docx.
  3. Use the prompt below to shape the deck.

Prompt · Generate Board deck

Create a Board update presentation from /02_Contoso_OpsReport.docx for the Q1 2025 Operations Review. Structure (10-12 slides): 1. Title slide — "Q1 2025 Operations Review · Contoso Plantation Berhad" 2. Executive summary 3. Plantation performance — group OER vs. target 4. Estate performance heatmap — top 3 / bottom 3 5. CRITICAL estates — Temerloh and Simalungun 6. Manufacturing — oleochemicals utilisation and margin 7. Property — KLK Land launches and take-up 8. Sustainability — RSPO / MSPO / EUDR status 9. Risks — top 3 with mitigations 10. Action items — owner, due date 11. Asks of the Board Use a green-and-cream palette, conservative corporate tone, no emojis.

What to notice

  • The summary is grounded in the source document — quotes, names and numbers come from the report.
  • Copilot can extract structured tables (action items) from unstructured prose (transcript).
  • The PowerPoint draft is a starting point, not a final — Siew Ling will refine wording, but the heavy lift of "blank deck → 80% draft" is gone.

Exercise 3 · Cowork ★

Centerpiece M365 Chat · ~15 minutes

📌 About Copilot Cowork

Cowork is the new M365 Copilot experience that lets you delegate multiple deliverables in a single prompt. Copilot drafts them in parallel as separate Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook and Teams artefacts — you review, refine, and ship.

Tenant note: Cowork must be enabled by your tenant administrator at the Microsoft 365 admin centre. If you don't see it yet, that's the lever — your IT team can switch it on.

🎯 Objective

In a single Cowork prompt, have Copilot prepare five Board-prep deliverables in parallel: an executive summary, a sustainability briefing note, a pre-Board email to division heads, a calendar invite for a follow-up review, and a Teams heads-up message to the Plantation Operations channel.

Steps

  1. Go to m365.cloud.microsoft and open Copilot.
  2. Switch to the Cowork experience (top of the chat panel).
  3. Attach 02_Contoso_OpsReport.docx as a reference.
  4. Paste the master prompt below.
  5. Watch Copilot fan out the work — five drafts arriving in parallel within a couple of minutes.
  6. Review each draft, refine in place, and save / send.

The master Cowork prompt

I am Lim Siew Ling, Group COO at Contoso Plantation Berhad. The quarterly Board Operations Review is on Friday. Using the attached Q1 Group Operations Report as the source of truth, prepare these five deliverables in parallel: 1. WORD DOCUMENT — "Q1 2025 Operations Executive Summary" • One page, board-grade, structured: Headline / Plantation / Manufacturing / Property / Sustainability / Top 3 Risks. • Calm, factual tone. ~350 words. 2. WORD DOCUMENT — "Sustainability Briefing Note · Q1 2025" • Two pages. • Cover RSPO recertification status, MSPO compliance, EUDR readiness for the EU Deforestation Regulation. • Highlight the Liberia operations gap and propose a remediation timeline. • End with three questions the Board should expect from the Sustainability Sub-committee. 3. EMAIL — to the four division heads (Plantation, Manufacturing, Property, Sustainability) • Subject line punchy and specific. • Reminds them the Board pack pre-reads close end of Wednesday. • Asks each to confirm two slides and one risk callout. • Warm but firm tone, signed "Siew Ling". 4. CALENDAR INVITE — "Liberia Operations Follow-up · Tuesday next week" • 60 minutes, 9:00 AM Kuala Lumpur time. • Invite the Plantation Director, Liberia Country Manager, and Group Sustainability Lead. • Agenda in the body: yield gap, EUDR readiness, capex pipeline, ask of the Board. 5. TEAMS MESSAGE — to the "Plantation Operations Leadership" channel • Three short paragraphs, conversational but professional. • Heads-up that Friday's Board review will spotlight Temerloh and Simalungun. • Asks Regional Heads to come prepared with a one-line operational hypothesis. • Ends on a positive note about Lahad Datu's strong quarter. Keep terminology consistent across all five artefacts: OER, FFB/ha, RSPO, MSPO, EUDR, KLK Land. Where you cite numbers, use the figures from the attached report.

What to watch for

⚡ Parallel drafting

Five artefacts in five different surfaces — Word ×2, Outlook email, Outlook calendar, Teams message — all from one prompt. Each is a real, editable artefact, not a chat blob.

🧭 Consistent grounding

Because all five reference the same Operations Report, the OER numbers, estate names and risk wording stay consistent across deliverables. That's the magic — no copy-paste drift.

🎯 Tone control

The Board summary is calm and factual; the Teams message is conversational; the email is "warm but firm". Cowork lets you set tone per artefact in one prompt.

🔁 Refine in place

Each draft opens in its native app. Edit the email directly in Outlook, tweak the deck in PowerPoint, adjust the briefing note in Word — Copilot stays available for follow-up edits.

The "before / after" for Siew Ling

Before Cowork

Five separate drafting sessions across two days — open Word, draft, save, attach, switch to Outlook, write the email, switch to Teams, write the message, schedule the meeting, brief the EA…

With Cowork

One prompt. Five drafts in parallel in under two minutes. The next 30 minutes is review and refinement — not first-draft drudgery. The team gets time back for what humans actually do best: judgement and conversation.

Exercise 4 · Agent Builder

Copilot Studio Lite · ~10 minutes

🎯 Objective

Build a lightweight "Plantation Advisor" agent grounded in the Group Operations Report. Anyone in the Plantation team can ask it questions in English or Bahasa Malaysia — and get answers anchored on the actual report, not the open internet.

Steps

  1. Open Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat.
  2. Click the Create agent icon (or go to Agents → Create).
  3. Name the agent Plantation Advisor.
  4. Paste the description below.
  5. Upload 02_Contoso_OpsReport.docx as the knowledge source.
  6. Add the suggested starter prompts.
  7. Test with the sample questions, then publish to the Plantation team.

Agent description

You are the Plantation Advisor — an internal assistant for Contoso Plantation Berhad's plantation operations team. Answer questions about Q1 2025 estate performance, OER trends, sustainability compliance (RSPO, MSPO, EUDR), and operational risks. Always ground your answers in the attached Q1 Group Operations Report. Where the report does not contain the answer, say so plainly. Audience: Estate Managers, Regional Heads, Plantation Director and the COO's office. Languages: respond in English by default; respond in Bahasa Malaysia when asked. Tone: factual, concise, professional. Use bullets and tables where helpful. Numbers: always quote the figure from the report and the section it came from.

Suggested starter prompts

  • "Which estates are CRITICAL this quarter and why?"
  • "Apa status persijilan RSPO untuk operasi Liberia?" (What is the RSPO certification status for Liberia operations?)
  • "Summarise the Q1 manufacturing margin story in three bullets."
  • "List every action item from the Q1 review, with owners."
  • "What's our exposure to the EU Deforestation Regulation?"

What to notice

  • The agent only answers from the uploaded document — try asking it "what's CPO trading at today?" and watch it decline.
  • It handles Bahasa Malaysia seamlessly — useful for Estate Managers who default to BM.
  • You can publish it to a Teams channel so the whole Plantation org has the same answers.
  • Building this took about 3 minutes end-to-end. No code, no Copilot Studio licence required.

Prompting Tips

A simple framework — and a plantation-flavoured prompt library.

The GCSE framework

Every good prompt has four parts. Get these four right and Copilot stops guessing.

Goal What do you want? Be specific about the output. "Draft a one-page executive summary" — not "Summarise this".
Context Who are you, who's the audience, what's the stakes? "I'm Group COO preparing for the Board."
Source What should Copilot ground the answer in? Attach a file, link a SharePoint doc, or paste the data in.
Expectation Format, tone, length, structure. "Calm, factual, ≤350 words, four bullets."

Worked example

I am Lim Siew Ling, Group COO at Contoso Plantation Berhad, preparing for the quarterly Board. Summarise the attached Q1 Operations Report as a one-page executive summary. Use only the figures and risks in the document — no external sources. Tone: calm, factual. Maximum 350 words. Structure: Headline / Plantation / Manufacturing / Property / Sustainability / Top 3 Risks.

Plantation prompt library

Excel · Yield analytics

Compare Q1 OER % across all Malaysian estates. Highlight any estate >2 percentage points below the group average. Return a table sorted ascending, plus a one-paragraph executive comment.

Word · Risk register entry

Draft a Group risk register entry for "EUDR non-compliance for Liberia operations". Use this structure: Risk description, Inherent likelihood, Inherent impact, Current controls, Residual rating, Owner, Next review date. Tone: formal, audit-ready.

Outlook · Estate visit follow-up

Draft a follow-up email to the Estate Manager of Temerloh, summarising the three operational hypotheses we discussed during my visit (canopy density, palm age profile, harvesting interval). Ask for a short response by Friday with which one he believes is the dominant driver. Warm but business-like. Sign as Siew Ling.

PowerPoint · Sustainability committee

Build a 6-slide deck for the Sustainability Sub-committee covering: RSPO recertification timeline, MSPO compliance, EUDR readiness, smallholder engagement progress, biodiversity programme highlights, asks of the Sub-committee. Conservative green/cream palette. No emojis.

Teams · Operations broadcast

Draft a short Teams broadcast to the Plantation Operations channel celebrating Lahad Datu's Q1 OER (21.3%, group leader), thanking the team, and gently signalling that next week's review will focus on Temerloh and Simalungun. Three short paragraphs.

Cowork · Quarterly close pack

Using the attached Q1 Group Operations Report, prepare in parallel: 1. WORD — one-page executive summary for the Board 2. WORD — sustainability briefing note (RSPO, MSPO, EUDR) 3. EMAIL — to division heads, agenda + pre-read reminder 4. CALENDAR — Liberia follow-up review, 60 min next Tuesday 9am MYT 5. TEAMS — heads-up to Plantation Operations Leadership channel Keep terminology consistent (OER, FFB/ha, RSPO, MSPO, EUDR). Calm board-grade tone for the docs; warm-but-firm for email; conversational for Teams.

🌱 One last tip

Treat Copilot like a smart graduate analyst who's just joined your team. They're fast, willing, and well-trained — but they need context and direction. Spend 30 seconds on a great prompt and you'll save 30 minutes on a great output.
